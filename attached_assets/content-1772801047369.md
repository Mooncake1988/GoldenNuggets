Hey fellow explorers,

I just got back from 11 days at Kunje Guest Farm. Usually, I’m only out there for a quick weekend, but this time I decided to extend my visit to 11 days.

It was harvest season, so the farm was incredibly busy. It was a good reality check and a reminder that there’s a whole world of work happening outside of sitting in front of a computer screen. People always say "go out and touch some grass," and I definitely did that. It made me realise how much things like Netflix and YouTube are often just a major distraction from actually being present.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,quality=80,format=auto,onerror=redirect/uploads/asset/file/631b673c-58c6-462c-9753-1768990857fb/Apples_at_Kunje_Fruit_Farm.jpeg)

### **The "Signs" Vibe**

One evening, I was walking through the fruit orchards at sunset. There was a slight wind blowing through the trees, and the atmosphere was just... eerie. It gave me that specific feeling of being in a scene from the movie _Signs_—it was actually a bit scary 👽. Do you remember the scene when Mel Gibson walks through the fields late at night with his flashlight?

### **Trails and Kindle Reading**

Living in Mouille Point, I’m usually out on the Promenade every day. But after a while the promenade gets boring. Out at Kunje, running through the orchards is a much more enjoyable workout and more peaceful when you’re the only person for kilometers.

> ![Instagram post](https://beehiiv-images-production.s3.amazonaws.com/uploads/asset/file/0e2278ca-3239-4a85-8507-a9995ccb2b0a/instagram_screenshot_DUsliR0jcVO_1772435833_3497c8d1.jpeg?t=1772435835)

The best part of the quiet, though, was the reading. I’m a big fan of the Kindle—I love being able to hear about a book and start reading it 30 seconds later, even in the middle of a fruit farm. I spent a lot of time by the pool reading through:

- **“Same as Ever" by Morgan Housel:** A great look at how human behavior stays the same even when the world changes.

- **"The 48 Laws of Power" by Robert Greene:** A classic I finally had the headspace to actually digest.


Having that much quiet time helped me think a lot more clearly about things like investment decisions. It’s amazing how much easier it is to be rational when you aren't being bombarded by notifications.
